About

What I Do

I write, learn, and contribute to the fields of Criminal Justice and Criminology. I am an aspiring scholar-in-training with plans of graduate education and eventual professorship at a research-intensive university. As a naturally argumentative devil’s advocate with a keen eye for detail and an interest for understanding things from a different perspective, academia is undoubtedly the right path for me.

I started college at fifteen years old due to my attendance at Florida Atlantic University High School (FAUHS), a rigorous dual-enrollment program that integrates students into the FAU campus. Here I was given a unique educational experience that broadened my horizons and have exposed me to research and areas of intrigue previously foreign to me.

Why I Do This

Through my research I’ve learned about problematic rates of case attrition and underreporting in the criminal justice system. I see how the criminal justice system is a unique and powerful institution. Criminal justice actors have the discretionary power to interpret the rules and punish those who break them. In essence, it has the power to regulate, control, and take away our liberties. Therefore, I see the importance of improving this institution to constantly seek ideal criminal justice practices. Beyond research, I also hope to make an impact mentoring the next generation of practitioners and scholars by affording them opportunities to learn about best practices in the criminal justice system.
